SHARK TANK ANALYSIS
What's the deal with the sharks!!
Shark Tank is an Emmy winning reality show that features a panel of potential investors, called "sharks," who consider offers from aspiring entrepreneurs seeking investments for their business or product. The show features 6 main sharks

1. Kevin O'Leary - A Tech Mogul
2. Barbara Corcoran - A Real Estate Businesswoman
3. Daymond John - Founder of FUBU apparel
4. Robert Herjavec - A dynamic IT entrepreneur
5. Mark Cuban - Investor
6. Lori Greiner - The Queen of QVC

This page provides an analysis of data from the first eight seasons of the show to answer the following questions

1. Are certain business category more attracted to the shark tank show?
2. What is the conversion rate of the participants in these business categories?
3. What is the average valuation of companies that close a deal?
4. Does the sharks have a favorite category in which they like to invest in?
About the Visualization
Narrative Structure
The narrative structure of the visualization follows an interactive slide show where the user can click on the next/previous buttons to go forward and backward within each slide. Each of the slide has an author driven path and the reader can drill down to analyse other data points on press of the explore button.

Scenes
The visualization has 4 scenes to answer each question cited in the previous page. All the scenes follow a consistent design and colors templates. The Author driven inferences for each of the questions are provided in the right sidebar. Additional links to the visualization are provided in each of the inferences on mouseover of highlighted keywords.

Annotations
There are number of annotation cue presented in the visualization. Each of the author driven charts provides red star symbols indicating the section of the chart that is discussed in the inferences. These red stars are cleared when the an user clicks on the explore button, in order to drill down further.
The first 3 charts also has additional annotations to provide context of the data in form of data summary and a donut chart. Tooltips and color also form basic annotation for all the charts.

Parameters
All slides uses the category as the nominal data and uses various domain values to generate the bar charts. Color is also passed as a parameter in the first chart to create a stacked bar chart. The first 3 charts also has a summary and a donut chart. The summary chart is generated through a function where the data elements are passed as parameters. The buttons and moveover of highlighted keywords in the inferences sidebar calls functions with the chart number as parameter to generate the appropriate chart.
Triggers The website navigation works on scrolling, press of up/down arrow and clicking of the right side navigation circles. The chart navigation works onclick the buttons (Prev,1,2,3,4,Next,Explore). Additional chart navigation is provided through the mouseeover of the highlighed keywords on the inferences. Placing buttons and highlights provides information to the user to click on the button and/or highlights. Within the chart, every chart has mouseover tooltip providing the raw data used in the chart. The first 3 charts also provides user triggers in the form of mouse click on the bar chart. On click of the bars provide the summary and donut chart to provide view on that particular category. Tooltip also provides information to the user to click.
The Analysis

1. Are certain business category more attracted to the shark tank show?
The first chart provides a clear evidence that the show attracts businesses from the Food, Fashion and Lifestyle industry. It is very evident that over 50% of the show participants are from these businesses.
2. What is the conversion rate of the participants in these business categories?
Businesses from Lifestyle and Fitness categories have the top deal closure conversion. However the other top categories are not very far behind them.
3. What is the average valuation of companies that close a deal?
Businesses from Media and Tech are highly valued companies in show. Entertainment/Media has the some of the most valuation in the companies that closed deals. Tech companies comes a far second.
4. Does the sharks have a favorite category in which they like to invest in?
The Shark Investment Chart clearly depicts the preference of the sharks. Lori invests in more lifestyle and Raymond invest more in Fashion. Chart also shows that Mark Cuban is more diverse in his investments.
